Horror Films in the Spotlight This Month

Oddity (2024)

When Dani is brutally murdered at the remote country house that she and her husband Ted are renovating, everyone suspects a patient from the local mental health institution, where Ted is a doctor. However, soon after the tragic killing, the suspect is found dead. A year later, Dani's blind twin sister Darcy, a self-proclaimed psychic and collector of cursed items, pays an unexpected visit to Ted and his new girlfriend, Yana. Convinced that there was more to her sister's murder than people know, Darcy has brought with her the most dangerous items from her cursed collection to help her exact revenge.

Released: July 19, 2024

Directed by: Damien McCarthy
Produced by: Laura Tunstall, Mette-Marie Kongsved, Katie Holly, Evan Horan
Production Companies: Keeper Pictures, Nowhere, Shudder
Starring: Gwilym Lee, Carolyn Bracken, Tadhg Murphy, Caroline Menton, JOnathan French, Steve Wall

Alexandra Heller-Nicholas of AWJF.org states:

"While the story of Oddity is a fascinating one, it is less the tale itself than the particular way McCarthy chooses to tell it that renders it one of the year’s best horror movies."


Katie Rife of IGN Movies reviews:

"Irish director Damian Mc Carthy shows great skill in creating uncanny atmosphere with an elegantly constructed tale that combines supernatural chills and violent revenge in the EC Comics tradition."

Tomatometer: 96% Certified Fresh

V/H/S/Beyond (2024)

Six bloodcurdling tapes unleash horror in a sci-fi-inspired hellscape, pushing the boundaries of fear and suspense.

Released: October 4, 2024

Directed by: Jay Cheel, Jordan Downey, Christian Long, Justin Long, Justin Martinez, Virat Pal, Kate Siegel
Produced by: Josh Goldbloom, James Harris, Michael Schreiber
Production Companies: Shudder Films, Studio71
Starring: Dane DiLiegro, Alanah Pearce, Mitch Horowitz

Sara Michelle Fetters of MovieFreak.com says:

"The V/H/S series happily shows no sign of slowing down, and V/H/S/Beyond is as thrilling an entry as any that has come before it. Just don’t plan on going skydiving anytime soon after watching."


Randy Myers of San Jose Mercury News reviews:

“'V/H/S Beyond' grossed me out, made me laugh and made me jump — and it set a high bar for ensuing entries to jump over."

Tomatometer: 90% Certified Fresh
